How is it still relevant today?

The question that you are probably wondering is how is this still relevant in this day and age?

Within those notes that she crafted she highlighted the difference between the Analytical Engine and previous calculating machines, she talked about how it can be programmed to solve problems that are more complex than any had done at the time [12]. She implicated what we do today on computers, she predicted what they will be and now they perform very complex problems. She realised something that her fellow peers, such as Babbage, did not. That was that engines aren’t just bound by numbers but that the number could represent entities that isn’t just a quantity [13]. They could represent letters, images or even music! This thought process was vital to be documented as it represented what the future can be.

It is what the future became as well! Think about how many can sit at home on their computers and write an email, watch a video or even listen to riveting lectures about Web Application!
